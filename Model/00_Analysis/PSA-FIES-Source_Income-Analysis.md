This is **Table 6** of the PSA FIES series, and it’s quite different from the previous tables. Instead of showing income levels or inequality measures, it breaks down **where family income comes from** – the percentage share of each income source for every region, province, and HUC.

### What the Table Shows

For each geographic area, you get:
- **2018, 2021, 2023p** – share (%) of total family income from 13 different sources.
- **Percentage point changes** (2021 minus 2018) and (2023 minus 2021).

The income sources include:
- **Wage/Salaries** (employment)
- **Entrepreneurial Activities** (own business, farming, fishing, etc.)
- **Cash Receipts from Abroad** (remittances from OFWs)
- **Cash Receipts from Domestic Sources** (transfers within the Philippines)
- **Rentals Received**, **Interest**, **Pension**, **Dividends**
- **Net Share of Crops/Livestock** (from other households)
- **Net Receipt from Family Sustenance Activities**
- **Received as Gifts**
- **Imputed Rent** (estimated value of living in own home)
- **Other sources**

### Key National‑Level Trends

| Income Source | 2018 | 2021 | 2023p | Change 2018→2021 | Change 2021→2023p |
|---------------|------|------|-------|------------------|------------------|
| **Wage/Salaries** | 50.46% | 52.72% | 56.11% | +2.26 pp | +3.39 pp |
| **Entrepreneurial** | 19.54% | 17.05% | 16.81% | -2.49 pp | -0.24 pp |
| **Cash Receipts from Abroad** | 9.57% | 7.16% | 7.83% | -2.41 pp | +0.67 pp |
| **Imputed Rent** | 8.28% | 9.70% | 8.63% | +1.42 pp | -1.06 pp |
| **Pension** | 2.90% | 3.05% | 2.49% | +0.15 pp | -0.56 pp |
| **Gifts** | 2.05% | 2.43% | 1.71% | +0.38 pp | -0.72 pp |

**What this tells us:**
- **Wage employment became much more important** – its share rose by almost 6 percentage points from 2018 to 2023. This reflects a shift away from self‑employment and entrepreneurship, possibly due to job formalization or recovery of salaried jobs after the pandemic.
- **Entrepreneurial income shrank** – from nearly 20% of total income to less than 17%. This could indicate business closures during the pandemic and a slower recovery for the self‑employed.
- **Overseas remittances fell sharply during the pandemic** (‑2.41 pp) but partially recovered (‑0.67 pp) – though still below 2018 levels.
- **Imputed rent** (the value of living in a home you own) rose in 2021 (likely due to more people staying home) then fell back.
- **Pension and gift shares** declined in the recovery period.

### Regional & City Highlights

- **NCR** relies much more on wages (66.5% in 2023p) and less on entrepreneurship (11.2%) compared to the national average. Remittances are also lower (4.6%).
- **BARMM** is the opposite: wage share is very low (25.9% in 2023p), while entrepreneurial activities dominate (52.3%). Remittances are also low (4.1%). This reflects a largely informal, agriculture‑based economy.
- **CAR (Cordillera)** had a large drop in entrepreneurial share (from 26.3% in 2018 to 19.3% in 2023) and a rise in wages (from 43.1% to 51.4%).
- **City of Makati** stands out for high dividend income (3.6% in 2023p) and higher‑than‑average rental income (1.3%).
- **Sulu** has an extremely high entrepreneurial share (74.7% in 2023p) and very low wage share (13.9%) – reflecting a largely subsistence economy.

### Important Caveats

- **2023 data are preliminary** and may be revised.
- **Region XII and BARMM** figures for 2018/2021 are **not comparable** with 2023 due to the transfer of 63 barangays from North Cotabato to BARMM. Any change for those areas is partly or entirely due to boundary changes, not real economic shifts.
- Percentages may not add to 100 due to rounding.

### In a Nutshell

This table answers the question: **Where do Filipinos get their money?** It shows a clear national trend toward wage‑based income and away from entrepreneurship, with remittances playing a smaller role than before the pandemic. Regional differences are stark – from wage‑dependent NCR to entrepreneur‑driven BARMM. It’s a valuable tool for understanding economic structure and how income sources have shifted over time.
