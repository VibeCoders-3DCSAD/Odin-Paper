### What These Metrics Mean

- **Gini coefficient** (0 = perfect equality, 1 = perfect inequality). Lower is more equal. The Philippines' Gini in 2023p was 0.3909, down from 0.419 in 2018, indicating income distribution became more equal over time.
- **Palma ratio** = share of income of the richest 10% divided by share of the poorest 40%. A value of 1 means the top 10% earn as much as the bottom 40%. The national Palma fell from 1.39 in 2018 to 1.15 in 2023p – a substantial drop, meaning the rich's income share fell relative to the poor.

### Key National Trend – Inequality Declined

| Year | Gini | Palma |
|------|------|-------|
| 2018 | 0.419 | 1.39 |
| 2021 | 0.406 | 1.26 |
| 2023p | 0.391 | 1.15 |

Both measures improved significantly, especially between 2021 and 2023p. This matches the earlier income tables where the poorest deciles saw faster income growth than the richest.

### Regional Contrasts – Some Areas Became More Equal, Others Not

- **Most equal regions** (lowest Gini in 2023p):
  - BARMM: 0.254 (very low, but note boundary change – not comparable across years)
  - Central Luzon (Region III): 0.307
  - NCR: 0.343

- **Most unequal regions** (highest Gini):
  - MIMAROPA: 0.405
  - Region XII: 0.434 (but not comparable due to boundary change)
  - Negros Island Region: 0.403

- **Biggest improvements** (Gini decline 2018→2023p):
  - CAR: from 0.447 to 0.353 (very large drop)
  - Region II: from 0.415 to 0.357
  - Region VIII: from 0.442 to 0.399

- **Areas where inequality worsened**:
  - City of Makati: Gini rose from 0.400 to 0.454 – became more unequal.
  - Sultan Kudarat (Region XII): Gini jumped to 0.535 in 2023p (but again, not comparable due to boundary change – may be misleading).

### Notable City-Level Observations

- **City of San Juan** (NCR): Gini rose from 0.417 (2018) to 0.422 (2023p), and Palma from 1.47 to 1.29 – still quite unequal, though Palma fell.
- **City of Makati** stands out as the most unequal among major NCR cities (Gini 0.454, Palma 1.68 in 2023p).
- **City of Caloocan** is very equal (Gini 0.262, Palma 0.52) – typical for lower-income areas.
- **City of Zamboanga** became much more equal (Gini 0.358→0.266, Palma 1.01→0.64).

### Important Caveats

- **Region XII and BARMM** figures for 2023p are **not comparable** with 2018/2021** due to the transfer of 63 barangays. The sharp changes in those areas (e.g., Sultan Kudarat's Gini jumping to 0.535) may be purely due to geographic redefinition, not real changes in inequality. Do not interpret those as genuine trends.
- 2023 data are preliminary.

### In a Nutshell

This table tells a clear story: **income inequality in the Philippines fell notably between 2018 and 2023**, especially after the pandemic. The Palma ratio dropped from 1.39 to 1.15, meaning the richest 10% now earn only 15% more than the poorest 40% (down from 39% more). Most regions and provinces followed this trend, though a few (like Makati) saw inequality rise. The BARMM region remains the most equal, though part of that is due to its very low average incomes. The table is a powerful complement to the income and expenditure tables, showing not just who gained but how the distribution shifted.
