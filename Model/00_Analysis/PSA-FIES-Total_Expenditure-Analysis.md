### What This Table Shows

- **Total spending** aggregated across all families in each area, not average per family.
- Allows comparison of how total consumption changed over time, accounting for both changes in spending per family and changes in the number of families.
- Includes the same geographic and decile breakdowns as the other tables.

### Key National‑Level Observations

| Year | Total Expenditure (all groups) | Poorest 10% (1st decile) | Richest 10% (10th decile) |
|------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| 2018 | 5,908,432 million | 270,141 million | 1,349,219 million |
| 2021 | 6,039,625 million | 274,226 million | 1,351,259 million |
| 2023p | 7,091,081 million | 351,316 million | 1,499,508 million |

- **Total national spending grew +2.2%** from 2018 to 2021 – much slower than total income growth (+4.5%) during the same period. This suggests families saved more during the pandemic.
- **From 2021 to 2023p, total spending jumped +17.4%** – slightly less than the +19.7% growth in total income, indicating the savings rate continued to rise.
- The **poorest decile** saw its total spending increase +28.1% from 2021 to 2023p, the fastest of any decile, consistent with income gains.
- The **richest decile** spending grew only +11.0% in the same period – much slower than their income growth (+14.5%), meaning the rich saved a larger share of their additional income.

### Regional Highlights

- **NCR** total spending fell -7.7% from 2018 to 2021 (from 1.22 trillion to 1.13 trillion), then rebounded +23.9% to 1.40 trillion in 2023p – a strong recovery.
- **CALABARZON** spending was flat from 2018 to 2021 (‑0.01%), then grew +16.6% to 1.34 trillion in 2023p.
- **BARMM** spending grew rapidly: +23.4% (2018–2021) and another +21.9% (2021–2023p), but note the boundary change footnote – 63 barangays added in 2023 make comparisons invalid across years.
- **Bicol Region (Region V)** spending grew slowly: +2.8% then +9.3%, lagging behind national averages.

### Interesting City‑Level Examples

- **City of San Juan** (NCR): total spending of the richest decile exploded from 3.5 billion in 2018 to 4.2 billion in 2023p (+63.6% from 2021!), indicating a massive concentration of high‑spending families.
- **Quezon City**: total spending of the top decile fell sharply from 2018 to 2021 (-7.8%), then rebounded only +20.0% – slower than many other NCR cities.
- **City of Makati**: richest decile spending grew +42.1% from 2021 to 2023p, reaching 31.3 billion PHP – one of the highest in the country.

### Relationship Between Tables 3 and 4

Comparing total income (Table 3) and total expenditure (Table 4) gives aggregate savings by decile and region. For example, nationally in 2023p:
- Total income = 9,706,613 million PHP
- Total expenditure = 7,091,081 million PHP
- **Aggregate savings = 2,615,532 million PHP** (about 27% of total income)

For the poorest decile:
- Total income = 393,686 million
- Total expenditure = 351,316 million
- Savings = 42,370 million (about 10.8% of their income)

For the richest decile:
- Total income = 2,460,818 million
- Total expenditure = 1,499,508 million
- Savings = 961,310 million (about 39% of their income)

This shows the rich save a much larger share of total income, even in aggregate.

### Important Caveats (Same as Previous Tables)

- 2023 data are **preliminary**.
- **Region XII and BARMM** comparisons across years are invalid due to the 63‑barangay transfer. Any percent changes for those areas should be ignored.
- Totals may not sum perfectly due to rounding.

### In a Nutshell

This spreadsheet completes the picture by showing **total spending** across the Philippines. It reveals that:
- Spending growth lagged behind income growth during the pandemic and recovery.
- The poorest decile increased spending rapidly in the recovery, but still saved a smaller share than the rich.
- Aggregate national savings rose from about 23% of income in 2018 to 27% in 2023p.
- Regional disparities in total spending mirror those in income, with NCR and CALABARZON dominating.
