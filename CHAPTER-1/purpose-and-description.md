# Purpose and Description of the Study

## Description of the Study

Filipino young professionals continue to face difficulty in maintaining consistent budgeting behavior despite increasing access to digital financial tools. Many existing budgeting applications are too generic in structure and do not adequately reflect the actual financial conditions of Filipino users, particularly those who manage variable income, family support obligations, recurring government contributions, debt repayments, and culturally specific community-based expenses. Because of this mismatch, available tools often function only as simple expense trackers rather than as intelligent systems that can guide users toward more informed and sustainable financial decisions. In response to this problem, this study proposes the development of Odin, a web and mobile personal budget management system designed specifically for Filipino young professionals in Metro Manila. The system will employ machine learning techniques to forecast spending behavior and detect unusual transaction patterns while also organizing users according to their financial characteristics through a behavioral profiling approach. The core strategy of the study is to align system functionality with the real financial structure of the target users by combining transaction monitoring, financial behavior profiling, predictive analytics, and rule-based budget guidance in a single platform. Key system features of Odin include financial behavioral profile assignment, transaction entry and categorization, spending forecast generation, anomaly detection alerts, budget recommendation support, savings goal tracking, long-term savings projection, and debt management guidance.

## Purpose of the Study

The primary purpose of this study is to design, develop, and evaluate Odin as a web and mobile personal budget management system for Filipino young professionals in Metro Manila. Specifically, the study aims to create a system that can help users manage their finances more effectively through spending forecasting, anomaly detection, and personalized budget guidance based on their financial behavioral profile. This purpose directly supports the proposed solution by addressing the limitations of conventional budgeting tools that do not adapt to the user’s income pattern, obligation weight, and actual spending behavior. Through the successful development and evaluation of Odin, the study seeks to demonstrate that a localized and behavior-aware budgeting system can provide more relevant and practical financial assistance to its intended users. The expected outcome of the study is a functional and usable system that supports informed budgeting decisions, improves financial monitoring, and offers intelligent assistance that is better aligned with the financial realities of Filipino young professionals.

## Significance of the Study

This study is significant because it addresses a practical and timely problem in personal financial management among Filipino young professionals. By developing a budgeting system that is tailored to local financial realities, the study offers a more context-sensitive alternative to existing tools that rely on generalized budgeting models. In practical terms, Odin may help users improve expense monitoring, identify unhealthy spending patterns, plan savings more effectively, and make more disciplined financial decisions. The system may also benefit students, employees, freelancers, and other young professionals who need accessible digital support in managing both fixed and variable financial obligations. From a technological perspective, the study is significant because it integrates spending forecasting, anomaly detection, and financial behavioral profiling within a single personal finance platform. This combination may serve as a useful model for future intelligent budgeting systems designed for specific user groups. Academically, the study contributes to the growing body of work on financial technology, decision-support systems, and intelligent personal finance applications by presenting a localized system design and evaluation framework that may be used as a reference for future studies in similar contexts.
