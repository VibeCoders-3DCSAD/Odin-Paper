| 1 | Financial Stress & Management (Filipino Millennials) | Lambert et al. (2025) | Various | Financial stress strongly reduces financial management (β = -0.724); millennial traits (tech-savvy, adaptability) improve it (β = 0.480). | 2 | Debt and Financial Literacy (OFWs) | Banta (2025) | Various | Financial literacy training for OFWs in Dubai individualizes debt, ignoring structural drivers (kafala system, family obligations, banking exclusion). | 3 | Aging Migrant Workers & Financial Self-Reliance | Amrith (2026) | Various | Only 5% of overseas Filipinos contribute to SSS. | 4 | Digital Finance Impact on Consumption & Savings | Askhiyah et al. (2026) | Various | Digital finance adoption increases consumption by 8.7%, reduces savings by 5.8%, raises debt-to-income by 6.4 percentage points. Financial literacy improves by 1.4 points. | 5 | Mental Accounting & Scarcity Mindset | Cheng et al. (2023) | Various | Windfall gains increase hedonic spending; scarcity mindset suppresses this effect. | 7 | Financial Literacy & Wellbeing (Youth Entrepreneurs) | Isaga (2025) | Various | Financial literacy directly improves financial wellbeing; financial behavior partially mediates this relationship. | Knowledge alone is insufficient; behavior change is the necessary pathway. | Tanzanian youth entrepreneurs; cross-sectional; self-reported. | Justifies emphasis on behavior change (budget adherence) over passive educational content. |
| 8 | Payday Effects & Mental Budgeting | Ma et al. (2026) | Various | Payday increases daily expenditure by 4.7% (salience effect). | 9 | Consumer Agency & Financial Ruin | Nokhiz & Ruwanpathirana (2025) | Various | Even rational consumers face ruin under obligatory payments, algorithmic manipulation, or income unpredictability. | 10 | Data-Driven Credit & Money Habits | Oyeyemi et al. (2025) | Various | Ensemble ML (XGBoost) outperforms logistic regression for credit classification.
