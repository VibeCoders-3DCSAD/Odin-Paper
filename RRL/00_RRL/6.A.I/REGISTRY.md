## I--Lee_et-al_2025

### Description

Compares deep learning architectures (LSTMs, CNN-LSTM hybrids) with traditional regression and tree-based models for individual-level healthcare cost prediction using real-world data.
- Deep Learning Accuracy Gains: Finds that sequential deep learning models consistently outperformed regression and tree-based algorithms in longitudinal designs, such as multi-year claims prediction and time-series forecasting, yielding 10–20% reductions in RMSE/MAE.
- Model Suitability by Data Type: Suggests that generalized linear models and tree-based approaches remain robust baseline models for low-dimensional, structured, cross-sectional medical data due to their interpretability and calibration stability, while deep learning excels in complex, sequence-rich data.
