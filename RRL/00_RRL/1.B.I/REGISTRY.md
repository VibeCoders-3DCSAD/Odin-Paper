## I--Duc_2026

### Description

Examines how platform workers in the Philippines and other ASEAN countries, who are classified as independent contractors, face disproportionate risks of income volatility compared to traditional employees.
- Irregular Platform Earnings: Notes that the irregular and piece-rate nature of platform earnings among gig workers, including youth and self-employed individuals in the Philippines, discourages voluntary, long-term savings for pensions.
- Individualized Income Risk: Discusses how the intrinsic volatility of platform work, subject to algorithmic management, demand surges, and market downturns, translates into a complete individualization of unemployment risk and income loss for these workers.

## L--Jandoc_et-al_2026

### Description

Examines non-traditional platform work, including gig labor, short-term contracting, part-time work, home-based work, and online freelancing, as new avenues for flexibility and income diversification for Filipinos.
- Explains Income Sources: Notes that the rise of flexible and non-traditional jobs is a response to the search for new income sources amid limited formal employment opportunities, reinforcing certain employment patterns.
- Discusses Income Instability: Highlights the vulnerability of gig and freelance workers to income shocks and job instability due to the lack of written contracts, paid leave, social insurance, or unemployment protection.
