## I--Agrawal_et-al_2025

### Description

Uses K-Means clustering to segment credit card users based on card types and spending behavior across specific categories, including luxury, travel, groceries, and EMIs (Equated Monthly Installments), which represent different levels of necessity or discretion.

## I--Cheng_et-al_2023

### Description

Examines the influence of mental accounting, specifically windfall gains versus hard-earning gains, on consumer decisions to spend on hedonic versus utilitarian consumption, demonstrating a distinction between these spending categories.
- Gain Source and Spending: Shows that consumers tend to use windfall gains for hedonic consumption and hard-earning gains for utilitarian consumption, suggesting a behavioral basis for classifying expenses by their source of funding.
- Scarcity Mindset Moderation: Reveals that the scarcity mindset moderates the effect of windfall gains on hedonic versus utilitarian consumption, indicating that psychological factors influence the meaningfulness of expense classifications.

## I--Pretnar_et-al_2025

### Description

Proposes a model that extends two-stage budgeting to incorporate frictions like budgetary stickiness and mental accounting, suggesting a structure for how consumers implicitly group expenses.
- Mental Accounting Structure: Suggests that inattention restricts the fungibility of money across mental accounts, implying that mental accounts serve as a high-level classification mechanism for spending.
- Evidence from Spending Data: Structurally estimates how much low-income consumers are constrained in their budgeting using agent-level weekly expenditure data, providing evidence that relates actual spending behavior to budgeting constraints.

## I--Simeonov_et-al_2025

### Description

Describes an approach to identify spending patterns in large financial transaction datasets using stochastic block modeling for community detection on a bipartite network.
- Consumer Spending Communities: Forms communities of consumers who share similar spending patterns across merchant categories, allowing for the identification of categories causing high carbon emissions for each group.
- Network Weight Modification: Introduces a modification to the weights of the bipartite network to keep the average community spending constant across different categories.
