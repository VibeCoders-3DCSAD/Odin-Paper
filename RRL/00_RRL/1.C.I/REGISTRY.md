## L--Am-Una_2026

### Description

Examines the financial behaviors of public school teachers in Baguio City, Philippines, finding that budgeting is widely practiced (most often done) but is considered the hardest financial task due to limited income.
- Budgeting Methods Used: Reports that budgeting behaviors among teachers were moderately positive, characterized by strong prioritization of bills but weak use of digital tracking tools for income and expenses.
- Predictive Behavioral Factors: Suggests that structural constraints, such as limited income and heavy debt burdens, shape teachers’ financial behaviors more than a lack of financial knowledge, with differences in behavior found by marital status and employment rank.

## L--Bongado_et-al_2025

### Description

Examines the financial behavior of Higher Education Institution (HEI) students in a State University of the Philippines, assessing it through cash management and financial planning and budgeting.
- Describes Budgeting Practices: Reports that students, including those of Generation Z, generally exhibit responsible financial practices and are actively applying budgeting and planning strategies in their daily lives.
- Budgeting Tools Used: Highlights that digital wallets are increasingly integral to daily transactions among students in the Philippine context, often serving as financial management tools that include budgeting templates and goal-setting features.

## L--Cervantes_et-al_2025

### Description

Examines the relationship between online buying behavior, influenced by factors like social media and instant gratification, and poor budget management among Filipino students.
- Impulsive Buying and Financial Control: Confirms a significant relationship between emotional and social triggers in online shopping and weakened financial control, suggesting a prevalence of poor budgeting habits among the sampled youth.
- Demographic Factors: Identifies demographic factors—most respondents were female students aged 18-24—who were highly responsive to digital trends and peer influence, and who struggled with resistance to unplanned purchases, leading to budgetary mismanagement.

## L--Claros_et-al_2026

### Description

Examines the saving behavior of 377 university students, aged 18 to 25, from private and public universities across different regions in the Philippines, who are emerging adults (Gen Z/Millennials) managing their personal allowances or income.
- Predictors of Saving Behavior: Identifies Financial Literacy, Parental Influence, and Self-Control as significant predictors of saving behavior among Filipino young adults, with Financial Literacy having the greatest impact.
- Parental Budgeting Influence: Suggests that parents practicing sound financial management, such as budgeting and regular savings, is a powerful mechanism for transferring these sound financial behaviors to their children.

## L--Espiritu_2025

### Description

Examines financial literacy practices, which include budgeting, among business administration students in an urban college setting in Novaliches, Quezon City, providing relevant context about young adults in Metro Manila.
- Demographic Factors on Practices: Notes that students' financial practices vary significantly when grouped according to demographic factors such as age, sex, monthly income, and year level, which can help predict who is more likely to budget.
- Links Budgeting to Knowledge: Explores the relationship between financial knowledge, attitudes, and financial practices, suggesting that budgeting behavior is linked to these other aspects of financial literacy.

## L--Espiritu_2026

### Description

Assesses financial decision-making processes, including budgeting practices, saving behavior, and spending patterns, among Financial Management students in a rural area of the Philippines.
- Digital Support for Budgeting: Reports descriptive statistics showing that respondents agree that online banking supports budgeting (overall mean score = 3.01), saving (overall mean score = 3.03), and spending management (overall mean score = 2.98).
- Correlates of Budgeting: Identifies transaction diversity and perceived trust and security in online banking as key correlates of stronger financial decision-making practices, which include budgeting, among young Filipino consumers.

## L--Flores_2025

### Description

Explains that financial literacy challenges, coupled with cultural tendencies like a "come-what-may" attitude and reliance on traditional saving methods, often impede effective budgeting, saving, and investment practices among Filipinos.
- Study Location and Sample: Focuses on the Metro Manila area, sampling 150 respondents from ten major corporations listed on the Philippine Stock Exchange Index, thus providing data from an urban Philippine setting.
- Focus on Young Professionals: Highlights that 17% of the respondents are in the 21–30 years of age bracket (representing a portion of Filipino millennials and Gen Z) and that the study aims to foster financial discipline, particularly among young professionals.

## L--Jumawan-Powao_et-al_2024

### Description

Indicates that the sampled Filipino accounting students often practice budgeting 51%–75% of the time with regard to fixed expenses, flexible expenses, and savings, suggesting a high level of budget literacy.
- Budgeting Method: Mentions the 50/30/20 rule of budgeting as a method used by many students to track monthly funds, allocating 50% for fixed expenses, 30% for flexible expenses, and 20% for savings, thus addressing budgeting formats.
- Predictive Factors: Finds that there is an indirect relationship between family income and budgeting, and that sex does not result in a significant difference in the budgeting practices of the participants, detailing demographic factors.

## L--Lambert_et-al_2025

### Description

Examines the influence of financial stress and generational characteristics on the financial management practices of Filipino millennials, including their budgeting, saving, and spending behaviors.
- Filipino Millennials Data: Analyzes data collected from 400 Filipino millennials to understand how they manage their family resources and finances, including practices like budgeting household income and expenses.
- Active Managers Studied: Focuses on millennials aged 20 to 40 who are actively managing their family resources, which includes the application of effective financial management skills such as planning and budgeting.

## L--Lim_&_Cordova_2024

### Description

Examines Filipino millennials' financial decision-making, including aspects of spending behavior and money management practices, using survey data from 431 millennials in Laguna, Philippines.
- Budgeting Variables Examined: Investigates budgeting as one of eight observed variables within the 'financial attitude' construct and one of ten observed variables within the 'financial efficacy' construct, to understand individuals’ beliefs and confidence related to budgeting and money management.
- Reported Budget Adherence: Reports that the majority of participants expressed positive financial attitudes, agreeing that they could manage their spending, set goals, save consistently, and stick to budgets, indicating a prevalent practice of budgeting or perceived ability to budget among the sample.

## L--Razalan_2024

### Description

Examines the money attitudes and financial well-being of Gen-Z individuals in Rizal province, Philippines, using a quantitative survey and qualitative methods.
- Budgeting Effort Among Gen-Z: Reports that Filipino Gen-Zs "demonstrate efforts to manage their finances through budgeting, saving, and planning when facing financial challenges."
- Specific Budgeting Behaviors: Indicates that Gen-Z individuals showcase their money attitude by "budgeting and prioritization" when managing finances, with working Gen-Zs specifically emphasizing "creating budgets, tracking their expenses and prioritizing their needs over wants."

## L--Sarmiento_et-al_2025

### Description

Examines the relationship between social media consumption of financial information and the financial literacy levels of students in Bulacan.
- Financial Information Source: Focuses on the financial guidance students in the Philippines seek and receive through social media, terming this practice "financial edutainment."
- Active Information Seeking: Reports that students were less inclined to actively seek out financial information, suggesting a lower prevalence of proactive financial behaviors like active budgeting among the surveyed population.
