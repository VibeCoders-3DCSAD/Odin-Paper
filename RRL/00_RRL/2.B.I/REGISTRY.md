## I--Ma_et-al_2026

### Description

Documents that besides making larger expenditures on a payday, customers also make larger expenditures on their first trip to a retail chain since a payday, even if it is a non-payday, followed by a decrease in per-trip expenditures over subsequent trips within the monthly paycheck cycle.
- Mental Budget Hypothesis: Suggests that the observed spending pattern—larger expenditures post-payday and decreasing expenditures during the cycle—implies that consumers may self-impose a monthly mental budget that is renewed on paydays.
- Magnitude of Fluctuations: Quantifies the payday effect for credit card users, showing it can shift up a customer's dollar-value daily consumption for the studied products by US$ 0.0212, which is a non-trivial magnitude relative to the average daily consumption rate of US$ 0.263.

## L--Canete_&_Liwanag_2024

### Description

Explores the intertwined concepts of “ubos biyaya” (depleted resources) and “petsa de peligro” (period before payday when financial constraints intensify) within the Filipino socioeconomic landscape.
- Causes of Financial Constraints: Examines 'petsa de peligro' as a consequence of 'ubos-biyaya,' connecting the cycle to the critique of capitalism, planned obsolescence, and consumption centers that contribute to resource depletion.
- Payday Spending Surges: Describes how Filipinos are commonly known for spending cash immediately upon payout, often indulging in shopping sprees and extravagant social activities, particularly at shopping malls on paydays (15th and 30th of the month).
