## TL;DR

Financial literacy improves financial wellbeing among youth entrepreneurs, with financial behaviour acting as a partial mediator.

## Problem and Motivation

The direct relationship between financial literacy and financial wellbeing is well-studied, but the mediating mechanism of financial behaviour remains underexplored, particularly among youth entrepreneurs in developing economies. Understanding this mediation is critical because interventions that only increase knowledge may fail without accompanying behavioural change. No prior study had explicitly tested the mediating role of financial behaviour in this specific population using structural equation modelling.

## Approach

- Dataset: 455 youth entrepreneurs in selected urban areas of Tanzania.
- Design: Cross-sectional, using structured questionnaires.
- Method: Structural Equation Modelling (SEM) to assess direct and indirect effects.
- Variables: Financial literacy (independent), financial behaviour (mediator), financial wellbeing (dependent).
- Analysis: Tested both direct and mediated paths.

## Findings

1. Financial literacy has a significant positive direct effect on financial wellbeing.
2. Financial literacy significantly and positively influences financial behaviour.
3. Financial behaviour **partially mediates** the relationship between financial literacy and financial wellbeing.

**Limits of relevance:**

- Study population is Tanzanian youth entrepreneurs, not Filipino young professionals; cultural and economic differences may affect the strength of mediation.
- No evaluation of digital or mobile financial tools — all data came from questionnaire self-reports.
- Cross-sectional design prevents causal claims about the direction of mediation.

## Limitations

- Cross-sectional design cannot establish causality; the observed mediation may be bidirectional or spurious.
- Self-reported financial behaviour is subject to social desirability and recall bias. [unacknowledged]
- No control for income level, education, or access to financial services — these may confound the mediation effect.
- Sample limited to urban areas; rural youth entrepreneurs may exhibit different behaviour patterns.

## Remember This

- 🔑 Financial behaviour partially mediates literacy→wellbeing — knowledge alone won't improve financial outcomes.
- 💡 Behaviour-focused interventions (budgeting, saving) matter more than just teaching finance.
- 📌 455 Tanzanian youth entrepreneurs in a cross-sectional SEM study — causal claims need longitudinal replication.
