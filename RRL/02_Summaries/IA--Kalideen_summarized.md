approach:
  - "Conducted a systematic literature search in IEEE Xplore, Scopus, and PubMed using keywords related to credit card fraud detection and machine learning."

  - "Classified ML techniques into supervised learning (logistic regression, SVM, decision trees, random forests, neural networks), unsupervised learning (clustering, anomaly detection, PCA), and semi-supervised/hybrid methods."
  - "Reviewed deep learning architectures: DNNs, RNNs (GRU), autoencoders, and GANs, highlighting their ability to capture non-linear patterns."
  - "Examined ensemble methods (bagging, boosting, random forests, stacking) for improving robustness and generalization."
  - "Compared evaluation metrics: accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, AUC-ROC, average precision, and Matthews correlation coefficient, noting unsuitability of accuracy for imbalanced data."
  - "Discussed challenges: imbalanced datasets, model interpretability (black-box problem), scalability for real-time detection, adversarial attacks, and privacy concerns."
  - "Explored emerging trends: explainable AI (XAI), privacy-preserving ML (federated learning, differential privacy, homomorphic encryption), and blockchain integration."
