## TL;DR

Financial literacy and fintech synergistically shape Gen Z's saving behavior; gamification and auto-reminders build discipline and reduce high-cost loan reliance.

## Problem and Motivation

Generation Z has high digital proficiency but low financial literacy, creating a gap between fintech usage and sound saving behavior. This gap undermines long-term financial resilience and increases dependence on costly debt. No prior systematic review synthesized how financial literacy and fintech together shape Gen Z's saving habits.

## Approach

- Systematic Literature Review (SLR) of peer-reviewed articles from Scopus, Emerald, Elsevier, MDPI, ProQuest, and Taylor & Francis (2020–2025).
- Keywords: “Financial Literacy,” “Digital Savings,” “Savings Behavior,” “Generation Z,” “Fintech” with Boolean operators.
- Initial pool of 250 articles; after title/abstract screening (180), full-text selection (120), eligibility analysis (90), final 6 high-quality articles.
- Inclusion criteria: specific focus on Gen Z and digital savings in fintech environment, clear methodology, peer-reviewed.
- Synthesis examined relationships among financial literacy, fintech features (auto-debit, reminders, gamification, balance transparency), and saving behavior outcomes.

## Findings

- Financial literacy provides the cognitive and affective foundation for healthy saving decisions among Gen Z.
- Fintech features (auto-debit, balance transparency, gamification) directly encourage savings discipline and reduce reliance on high-cost loans.
- Synergy between financial literacy and fintech strengthens sustainable saving behavior and long-term financial resilience.
- Fintech use explains **72%** of variation in Gen Z's financial literacy levels (Hastuty Hs et al., 2024).
- Digital payments in Indonesia projected to grow >67% by 2028, indicating expanding fintech adoption.

**Limits of relevance:**

- Paper is a systematic literature review, not primary empirical research; specific effect sizes (e.g., 72%) are drawn from individual studies, not meta-analyzed.
- Focus on saving behavior, not directly on budget adherence or spending forecasting; translation to Odin's modules requires analogical reasoning.
- Fintech environment in the reviewed studies often assumes banking integration (auto-debit from accounts), whereas Odin relies on manual input only.
