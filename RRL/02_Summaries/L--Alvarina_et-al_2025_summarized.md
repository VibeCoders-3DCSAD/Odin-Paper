## TL;DR

Below-minimum-wage sales personnel in Pagadian City prioritize essentials (food, utilities, childcare) and supplement income through side hustles, demonstrating tight budgeting and emergency savings despite financial stress.

## Problem and Motivation

Many Filipino workers earn below minimum wage, but their specific budgeting practices remain understudied. Understanding these practices is essential for designing effective financial support policies and improving economic well-being. Prior research focused on general low-income finance without examining the actual allocation strategies and coping mechanisms of sales personnel in local enterprises.

## Approach

- Qualitative case study of 10 below-minimum-wage sales personnel in Pagadian City.
- Semi-structured interviews with five open-ended questions on income sources, budget allocations, challenges, coping mechanisms, and insights.
- Thematic analysis applied to transcribed interview data.
- No quantitative baselines or statistical comparisons.

## Findings

1. Income sources: salary (10/10) supplemented by government aid (5), online selling (3), selling thrift items (1), side dishes (1).
2. Budget allocations prioritize **10/10 participants allocate to childcare**, 9/10 to food, 6/10 to utilities, 3/10 to rent, 2/10 to daily allowance.
3. Challenges: managing multiple expenses (5/10), limited healthcare access (3/10), insufficient savings (2/10).

- Coping mechanisms: tight budgeting and prioritizing essential costs.
- Positive insights: tracking expenses (4), utilizing resources (4), creating emergency funds (3).

## Limitations
