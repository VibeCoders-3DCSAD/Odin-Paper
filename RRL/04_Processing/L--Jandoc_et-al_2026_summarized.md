1.A.I
Presents a detailed profile of workers in non-traditional platform-mediated work using nationally representative data from the 2025 Jobs and Skills Survey (JSS), which was administered as a rider to the Labor Force Survey (LFS).
- Platform Worker Demographics: Indicates that platform workers are disproportionately young, urban, and highly educated, with a strong geographic concentration in the National Capital Region (NCR) and major urban corridors.
- Dominant Employment Sectors: Notes that platform workers are engaged in sectors such as online freelancing, outside-home services, ride-hailing/passenger transport, and delivery/task-based services.

1.B.I
Examines non-traditional platform work, including gig labor, short-term contracting, part-time work, home-based work, and online freelancing, as new avenues for flexibility and income diversification for Filipinos.
- Explains Income Sources: Notes that the rise of flexible and non-traditional jobs is a response to the search for new income sources amid limited formal employment opportunities, reinforcing certain employment patterns.
- Discusses Income Instability: Highlights the vulnerability of gig and freelance workers to income shocks and job instability due to the lack of written contracts, paid leave, social insurance, or unemployment protection.
