## I--Banta_2025

Title: Caring for indebted migrant workers: financial literacy training, debt, and Filipino migrant workers in Dubai

Author/s: Banta

Description:
Explores the politics of care in state and private initiatives assisting overseas Filipino workers (OFWs) in Dubai, including financial literacy training programs.
- Literacy and Debt: Examines how financial literacy education, framed as care, can obscure the political economic and structural conditions that create and maintain debt among Filipino migrant workers.
- Debt Among OFWs: Focuses on the rising levels of debt among professional, skilled, and semiskilled overseas Filipino workers in Dubai despite efforts to turn them into "financially mature" individuals.

## L--Claros_et-al_2026

Title: Determinants of Saving Behavior Among Filipino University Students: A Psychological and Social Perspective

Authors:
- Jericho Rey Claros
- Joaquin Angelo Gaza
- Zunia Anne Villaverde
- Imelda T. Angeles

Description:
Examines how financial literacy, parental influence, peer impact, and self-control affect saving behavior among Filipino university students, who represent a significant portion of the young adult demographic.
- Financial Literacy Boosts Saving: Reveals that financial literacy had the greatest positive impact on saving behavior, contradicting the premise that higher literacy does not translate into disciplined behavior in this study's context.
- Self-Control Negatively Affects Saving: Finds that, unexpectedly, self-control negatively affects saving behavior, challenging the conventional assumption about the interplay of discipline and action and suggesting a potential gap between discipline and actual saving action.

Authors:
- Ramon George Atento, PhD
- Cherry Ann Marie Espelita, MBA
- LiJuan Rao, PhD
- Yue Tian, MBA

Description:

## L--Flores_2025

Title: Financial freedom of Filipinos in personal finance management

Authors:
- Christian Anthony Ruiz Flores

Description:
Reveals pervasive financial literacy challenges among Filipinos, including young professionals, suggesting a low documented level of knowledge among the general population studied.

## L--Lim_&_Cordova_2024

Title: Decoding the eco-financial mindset: financial literacy, attitudes, and efficacy measures and the spending behavior of Filipino millennials

Authors:
- Cristina Teresa Lim
- Wilson Cordova

Description:
Found Filipino millennials in Laguna, Philippines, to have a moderately knowledgeable overall financial literacy level (AVE = 3.42), showcasing a solid understanding of topics like interest rates and general financial knowledge.
- Attitude-Behavior Correlation: Identified a negative correlation between spending behavior and financial attitude, suggesting that a more positive financial attitude leads to more responsible spending behaviors.
- Knowledge-Behavior Gap: Did not uncover a statistically significant relationship between spending behavior and financial literacy, which is evidence of a gap between financial knowledge and actual spending behavior.

## L--Mutuc_2025

Title: Exploring the Interplay of Life Attitude and Cognitive Ability in Shaping the Intention to Stock Market Participation Among Young Professionals in the Philippines

Authors:
- Eugene Burgos Mutuc

Description:
Aims to quantify the cognitive ability, specifically financial literacy and related competencies, of young Filipino professionals that influence their information processing and investment decision-making.
- Financial Literacy Reinforces Behavior: Finds that cognitive ability, represented by financial literacy, acts as a critical mechanism that reinforces the relationship between a positive life attitude and higher propensity to invest in the stock market, implying that knowledge complements psychological readiness for informed financial decisions.
- Behavioral Finance Context: Demonstrates that financial behavior among young Filipino professionals is not exclusively the product of economic rationality, but is profoundly shaped by psychological factors (life attitude) and cognitive competence (financial literacy).

## L--Rodriguez_et-al_2024

Title: The Mediation of Financial Behavior toFinancial Literacy and Spending Habitsof Gen Z: An Exploratory Factor Analysis

Authors:
- Joel Mark P. Rodriguez
- Ma. Del Carmen G. Labong
- Lourdes Q. Palallos

Description:
Explains the direct and indirect impacts of financial literacy on spending behavior, using financial behavior as a mediator among Generation Z respondents.
- Measured Relationships: Finds that financial literacy has a strong direct relationship with spending behavior (coefficient 1.167), while financial behavior has a strong mediation effect (coefficient 0.797).
- Data and Measurements: Gathers data from 317 respondents using a structured questionnaire that measures financial literacy, financial behavior, and spending behavior.

## L--Sanchez_2024

Title: Motivational Factors and Behavioral Intention to Invest in Philippine Stock Market among Millennial and Gen-Z Investors in Calamba City

Authors:
- Mark Quiel C. Sanchez, CPA, MBA

Description:
Explores the motivational factors, including financial knowledge and financial well-being, influencing the behavioral intentions of Millennial and Gen-Z investors in the Philippine stock market, specifically in Calamba City.
- Correlation to Behavior: Reveals that while participants agreed on key motivational factors (including financial knowledge and well-being), there was only a low positive correlation toward behavioral intention for investing, suggesting a disconnect between knowledge/well-being and action.
- Aspirations vs. Status Gap: Highlights a documented gap between the financial aspirations of Millennial and Gen-Z investors and their current financial status.
