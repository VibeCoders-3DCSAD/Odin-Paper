Title: Theorizing Labor in the Platform Economy: Labor Restructuring in Historical Perspective

Author/s: Faraoun

Description:
Discusses how Filipino content moderators working in commercial content moderation (a form of outsourced platform labor) understand their status as “on-demand labor” and interpret it as a means to achieve middle-class status and success.
- Platform Labor in Philippines: Mentions research on commercial content moderation in the Philippines, noting that off-shore firms managing a fragmented workforce use various engagement mechanisms to attract skilled urban youth for platform labor.
- Classifying Platform vs. IT Labor: Distinguishes platform labor, which is often understood as the expansion of the on-demand gig economy with short-term, precarious workers, from the labor of creating and running platforms (like app developers and IT professionals).

Title: Digitalisation and Labour Markets in  Developing Countries

Author/s: Fietz & Lay

Description:
Reviews the evidence on the effects of digitalization on labor markets in developing economies, including the Philippines, by focusing on digital platforms, which often involve gig-work and online freelancing.
- Employment Status Classifications: Distinguishes between various employment statuses relevant to the research question, including salaried workers, self-employed individuals, informal workers, freelancers, and those involved in the gig economy or platform work.
- Filipino Job Trends: Notes that fast-growing job profiles in the Philippines and Indonesia include managing online content and social media, indicating a demand for digital skills among young professionals and adults.

Title: Financial literacy and financial wellbeing of youth entrepreneurs: The mediating role of financial behaviour

Author/s: Isaga

Description:
Examines the relationship between financial literacy and financial well-being, focusing on the mediating role of financial behavior (budgeting, saving, debt management) among youth entrepreneurs in Tanzania.
- Income Instability and Behavior: Highlights that youth entrepreneurs often operate with unstable income streams and lack formal financial education, making financial behavior critical for translating knowledge into improved financial outcomes and long-term stability.
- Call for Philippine Research: Suggests that future research should investigate rural and informal entrepreneurial settings to capture the financial realities of underserved populations in the Philippines, despite the main study focusing on Tanzania.
