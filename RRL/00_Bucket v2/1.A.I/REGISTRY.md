Title: Gender equality, disability, and social inclusion in the Philippines: Progress, challenges, and opportunities in SDG 5 and SDG 10

Author/s: Albert et al.

Description:
Utilizes quantitative data from multiple waves of national surveys, including the Labor Force Survey (LFS) and the Family Income and Expenditure Survey (FIES), to examine employment patterns and economic information for working-age adults (15-64 years), which includes the target demographic of young professionals.
- Includes Formal Employment Data: Incorporates data from major administrative sources, such as the Government Service Insurance System (GSIS) and Social Security System (SSS), to provide insights into formal sector employment and social protection access among different population groups.
- Examines PSA Data Sources: Draws on data from the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), specifically the SDG Watch Database, Annual Poverty Indicators Survey (APIS), and the Family Income and Expenditure Survey (FIES), which are the requested institutional sources for population and household economic data.

Title: Profiling Platform Workers in the Philippines: Evidence from the Jobs and Skills Survey

Author/s: Jandoc et al.

Description:
Presents a detailed profile of workers in non-traditional platform-mediated work using nationally representative data from the 2025 Jobs and Skills Survey (JSS), which was administered as a rider to the Labor Force Survey (LFS).
- Platform Worker Demographics: Indicates that platform workers are disproportionately young, urban, and highly educated, with a strong geographic concentration in the National Capital Region (NCR) and major urban corridors.
- Dominant Employment Sectors: Notes that platform workers are engaged in sectors such as online freelancing, outside-home services, ride-hailing/passenger transport, and delivery/task-based services.
