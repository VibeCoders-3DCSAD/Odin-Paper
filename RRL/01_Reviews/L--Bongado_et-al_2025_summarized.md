## TL;DR

Digital wallet usage explains 45.4% of variance in financial behavior among Filipino HEI students.

## Problem and Motivation

The influence of digital wallets on students’ financial behavior (cash management, budgeting) is underexplored, especially in the Philippines. Prior studies show mixed effects — convenience and speed versus impulsive spending and weakened planning. This gap matters because digital wallets are now integral to daily student transactions, yet their net impact on responsible financial management remains uncertain.

## Approach

- Quantitative descriptive‑correlational design with 219 randomly selected HEI students in Talisayan, Misamis Oriental.
- Validated survey based on the Technology Acceptance Model (TAM), measuring perceived ease of use, usefulness, value, social influence, trust, security, and intention to use.
- Financial behavior measured via cash management and financial planning & budgeting (4‑point Likert scale).
- Regression analysis (Pearson product‑moment correlation) used to assess influence of digital wallet usage on financial behavior.

## Findings

1. Overall digital wallet usage mean = **2.95** (Agree), SD = 0.88.
2. Financial behavior overall mean = **2.99** (Agree), SD = 0.68.
3. **R² = 0.454** — digital wallet usage explains 45.4% of variance in financial behavior.
4. Standardized coefficient β = 0.673, p < .001 — significant positive influence.
5. Perceived trust had the highest variability (SD = 1.24), indicating divided opinions on platform reliability.

## Key Figures and Tables

- Table 1 (Determinants of digital wallet usage): Perceived trust shows SD=1.24 → most contentious factor.
- Table 2 (Financial behavior): Financial planning & budgeting (M=3.00, SD=0.84) → students are slightly proactive.
- Table 3 (Regression analysis): R²=0.454, β=0.673, p<.001 → strong positive effect.

## Key Equations

$$FinancialBehavior = 0.909 + 0.703 \times DigitalWalletUsage$$
*Each unit increase in digital wallet usage raises financial behavior score by 0.703 (standardized β=0.673).*

**Limits of relevance:**

- Does not address expense categorization, anomaly detection, or long‑term forecasting.

## Limitations

- Self‑reported survey data — subject to social desirability and recall bias.
- Single university campus — generalizability to other Philippine regions or institutions unknown.
- No control for prior financial literacy level, which may moderate the effect.
- Year of publication unknown (year=0000) — recency of findings uncertain.
