## TL;DR

Digital payments reached 57.4% of retail transaction volume and 59.0% of value in 2024, driven by merchant payments and P2P transfers.

## Problem and Motivation

The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) needs to measure the shift from cash to digital channels to guide policy and track financial inclusion. Before this measurement framework, no unified model quantified digital payment adoption across government, business, and person categories. Reliable data helps identify barriers and opportunities for digital transformation.

## Approach

- Use a 3×3 payment grid covering government (G), business (B), and person (P) as payers and payees.
- Identify 24 payment use‑cases (e.g., G2B, B2P, P2B) and estimate monthly volumes/values.
- Combine administrative data, stakeholder interviews, and surveys from sources like DBM, BIR, PSA, and PCHC.
- Apply assumptions to fill data gaps, then compute digital share per use‑case.

## Findings

1. Digital payments accounted for **57.4%** of total monthly retail volume (up from 52.8% in 2023) and **59.0%** of value.
2. Merchant payments (P2B) drove growth: 2.2B transactions (66.4% of digital volume), up 29.1% year‑on‑year.
3. P2P transfers grew 34.7% to 680.5M transactions (20.6% share), while B2B supplier payments reached 205.0M transactions (6.2% share). Government disbursements are nearly fully digital (97.2% by volume, 97.2% by value). Person‑to‑government (P2G) payments remain low: only 24.6% digital by volume, 41.6% by value.

## Limitations

- Estimates rely on assumptions and data gaps (e.g., informal cash transactions not fully captured).
- No breakdown by age or income group, so cannot specifically isolate young professionals.
- Value figures in USD are likely converted from PHP; exchange rate fluctuations not discussed.
- Does not measure user satisfaction, security perceptions, or adoption barriers beyond volume/value.
