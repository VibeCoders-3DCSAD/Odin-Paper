## TL;DR

Medium-income Nigerian households use 13 budgeting techniques but face knowledge gaps and irregular income as major barriers.

## Problem and Motivation

Nigerian households suffer severe financial difficulties due to rising living costs and economic volatility. Prior literature lacked empirical data on specific budgeting strategies used by medium-income households in Obio-Akpo LGA. Understanding these tactics is necessary to design interventions that increase financial resilience.

## Approach

- Survey of 600 medium-income households in Obio-Akpo LGA, Rivers State, Nigeria.
- Multi-stage sampling; 500 valid responses (93.3% return).
- 4‑point Likert scale questionnaire (strongly agree to strongly disagree).
- Data analyzed using mean and standard deviation; cutoff mean ≥2.50 = agreed.

## Findings

1. **11 income sources** available, including crop farming (X̄=3.50), food/catering services (X̄=3.67), and waste disposal (X̄=3.46).
2. **13 budgeting techniques** utilized, led by tracking all income sources (**X̄=3.66**) and using budgeting tools (X̄=3.43).
3. **13 challenges** to budgeting: lack of knowledge (X̄=3.47), absence of family/community support (X̄=3.45), and emergencies (X̄=3.14).
4. **17 improvement strategies**: access to financial advice (X̄=3.66), health coaching (X̄=3.44), and tutorial services (X̄=3.55).

## Limitations

- Self‑reported survey data may contain social desirability and recall bias.
- Cross‑sectional design captures practices at one point, not longitudinal behavior.
- Sample limited to one LGA in Rivers State; generalizability to other Nigerian regions or countries is untested.
- No qualitative follow‑up to explain why specific budgeting techniques fail or succeed.
