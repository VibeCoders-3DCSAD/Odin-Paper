## TL;DR

Qualitative study of 53 Hong Kong ethnic minority young adults reveals common budgeting, saving, and fraud‑detection behaviors, enabled by family and peer support.

## Problem and Motivation

Day‑to‑day financial decisions (budgeting, saving, fraud detection) of younger racial/ethnic minorities are underexamined, and most studies are quantitative and Western‑centric. Without this understanding, financial literacy programs remain untailored. This paper provides an in‑depth qualitative account of ethnic minority young adults in a non‑Western context (Hong Kong).

## Approach

- Semi‑structured interviews with **53** Pakistani, Indian, Nepalese, and Filipino participants aged 18–29.
- Recruited via NGOs serving ethnic minority youth and university international offices.
- Covered six areas: personal budgeting, spending, financial planning, use of financial products, debt management, fraud detection.
- Thematic analysis following Braun & Clarke (2006) to extract practices, strategies, and perceptions.

## Findings

1. **One‑third of income saved** – most participants saved at least 30% of monthly earnings using tactics like separate accounts or parental monitoring.
2. **One‑third owned a credit card** – most paid on time, but ignorance of interest charges led some to pay only the minimum.
3. Informal borrowing from family/friends was common; government and high‑interest lender loans were also used.
4. Fraud victimization occurred despite protective tactics (ignoring suspicious calls, pretending no Cantonese).
5. Enabling factors: family social capital, intrapersonal characteristics (self‑discipline, future orientation), peer support, knowledge proficiency, and facilitative circumstances (employment, pandemic lessons).

## Definitions

| Term / Acronym | Plain-English Definition |
| -------------- | ------------------------ |
| YOLO spending | “You Only Live Once” – spending for present happiness rather than strict saving. |
| Mental accounting | Cognitive tendency to treat money differently based on source or intended use, often leading to suboptimal decisions (e.g., stashing cash under mattress). |
| Financial socialization | Process of developing values, norms, and behaviors for financial well‑being, primarily through parents and peers. |
| MPF | Mandatory Provident Fund – Hong Kong’s compulsory pension scheme. |
| KOL | Key Opinion Leader – influencer (e.g., on YouTube) whose advice may be unreliable. |

## Limitations

- Sample well‑educated and English‑proficient; may not represent lower‑socioeconomic ethnic minorities.
- Small qualitative sample (n=53) — not generalizable to all ethnic minority young adults.
- All participants from Hong Kong; findings may not transfer to Philippine or other Southeast Asian contexts.
- No longitudinal data — captures behaviors at a single point in time.
