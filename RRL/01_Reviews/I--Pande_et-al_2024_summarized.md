## TL;DR

Scholarly interest in personal financial planning surged after 2010, with the US leading publications (62) and behavioural finance and FinTech as dominant themes.

## Problem and Motivation

No comprehensive systematic review of personal financial planning literature over the past two decades existed. Key drivers (behavioural factors, technology, financial literacy) and theoretical foundations remained fragmented. Policymakers and practitioners need consolidated evidence to design effective financial regulations and tools.

## Approach

- Scopus database search from 2004 to 2024 using keywords “Personal Financial Planning”, “Financial Wellness”, and “Financial Management”.
- PRISMA framework for inclusion/exclusion, filtering 310 initial documents to 158 final English articles.
- Subjects: Economics, Finance, Business, Social Sciences. Document types: articles, conference papers, reviews, editorials.
- Analysis: temporal trends, journal productivity, institutional contributions, country distribution, and content analysis.

## Findings

1. **US produced 62 publications** (highest), followed by India (19) and Malaysia (18).
2. Publications grew slowly before 2010 (<5/year) then accelerated, peaking in 2017, 2019, 2020, and 2023.
3. Journal of Financial Counselling and Planning is the most productive (17 documents).
4. Kansas State University leads institutional output (9 papers); Ohio State (8).
5. Dominant themes: behavioural finance, digital financial planning tools, and financial literacy.

## Limitations

- Scopus-only search may miss relevant publications from other databases.
- English-only papers exclude non-English research, potentially biasing toward Western contexts.
- Self-reported data in reviewed studies introduces social desirability bias.
- Cross-sectional designs in source papers prevent causal inference.

## Remember This

- 💡 Publications **accelerated after 2010** — field is young and fast-growing.
