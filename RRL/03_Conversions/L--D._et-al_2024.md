3. Effects of Inflation
Inflation  aims  to  measure  the  overall  impact  of  price
changes  for  a  diversified  set  of  products  and  services.  It
allows for a single value representation of the increase in the
price  level  of  goods  and  services  in  an  economy  over  a
specified  time.  In  an  atmosphere  of  inflation,  unevenly
increasing  costs  ultimately  diminish  some  customers'
purchasing power, and this loss of real income is the single
most expensive aspect of inflation. Inflation can also distort
long-term purchasing power for fixed-interest rate receivers
and payers.
