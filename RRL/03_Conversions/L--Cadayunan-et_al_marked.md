A Descriptive-Correlational Analysis of Social Media Usage and
Personal Finance Competencies Among University Students

 Alpha Mae G. Cadayunan, Areison Clark M. Fabe, Glydel T. Tingabngab,
Shaira Mae D. Bagtong
Bukidnon State University

Abstract

Social  media  plays  a  major  role  in  the  lives  of  young  adults,  yet  there  is  a  lack  of

understanding about how it influences financial  competencies among university students in the

Philippines. This study employed a descriptive correlational design to determine the relationship

between social media usage and personal finance competencies among students at Bukidnon State

University  Talisayan  Campus.  Key  findings  indicate  that  social  media  usage  is  high,  with

Facebook  and  TikTok  being  the  most  popular  platforms.  Financial  literacy  was  found  to  be

moderate, suggesting that students grasp financial concepts but struggle to apply them. The study

also demonstrated a moderate positive correlation between social media use and financial literacy,

as  well  as  decision-making  skills.  The  researchers  recommend  that  universities  incorporate

practical  financial  education  into  their  curricula,  utilize  social  media  for  financial  literacy

campaigns, and offer tools to help students effectively manage their finances.

Keywords:  social  media,  financial  literacy,  decision-making,  personal  finance,  university
students, financial education.
