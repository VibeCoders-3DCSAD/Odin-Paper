Abstract
Impulse buying is a widely studied consumer behavior influenced
by various psychological, social, and marketing factors. This study
aims to identify the key drivers of impulse buying within the digital
shopping environment. Utilizing the Stimulus-Organism-Response
(SOR) framework, this research examines the effects of age, gen-
der, social factors (peer influence, word of mouth), marketing fac-
tors (discounts), consumer characteristics (income level, shopping
lifestyle), and website-related factors (ease of use, ease of payment)
on consumer purchasing decisions. Additionally, the study assesses
the mediating role of urgency to buy and explores the cultural and
financial influences shaping impulse purchasing behavior in the
Filipino market. A quantitative research design was employed,
utilizing survey data collected from 381 respondents. Findings indi-
cate a significant relationship between social and website-related
factors in driving impulse purchases. Specifically, word of mouth
and peer influence enhance website appeal, while ease of use and
payment options increase the likelihood of unplanned purchases.
Results suggest that the increasing accessibility of online shopping
platforms has intensified impulse buying tendencies. This study
provides valuable insights for businesses and digital marketers, em-
phasizing the importance of optimizing website design, integrating
customer reviews, and streamlining payment processes to enhance
consumer engagement. The findings contribute to the growing body
of literature on impulse buying behavior in e-commerce and serve
as a foundation for future research on consumer decision-making
in digital environments.

1 INTRODUCTION
Impulse buying is a spontaneous purchasing behavior influenced
by social, marketing, and website-related factors. Consumers often
make unplanned purchases due to emotional triggers, external
stimuli, or promotional strategies. Research indicates that 60% of
purchases result from impulse buying, highlighting its significant
role in consumer decision-making [1].

With the rise of e-commerce and digital marketing, impulse
buying has evolved. Consumers are influenced by peer recom-
mendations, personalized ads, and website features that enhance
shopping convenience [4]. The COVID-19 pandemic further inten-
sified this behavior, as individuals turned to online shopping for
stimulation and social connection [22]. In the Philippine market,
impulse buying is also shaped by cultural and social influences,
including peer pressure and financial capability [19].

This study examines the key drivers of impulse buying, focusing
on the impact of social, marketing, and website-related factors. By
understanding these influences, businesses can optimize digital mar-
keting strategies, enhance website usability, and tailor promotions
to encourage impulse purchases. These insights contribute to the
growing research on consumer behavior in the digital marketplace.
With the rapid evolution of e-commerce and digital marketing,
this study examines the key factors driving impulse buying in online
shopping. Factors such as discounts, peer influence, and website
usability play a significant role in shaping consumer behavior [9].
The study aims to understand how these elements contribute to the
shift from impulse buying in physical stores to online platforms,
providing insights into optimizing marketing strategies and en-
hancing consumer engagement. To achieve this, the research seeks
to answer the following questions:

The Stimulus-Organism-Response (SOR) framework explains
how external stimuli, such as marketing strategies, website design,
and promotions, influence consumer behavior through internal
cognitive and emotional processing, leading to impulse buying [6]
[12]. Originally derived from the stimulus-response model, the SOR
theory integrates stimuli, organism responses, and behavioral out-
comes to provide a deeper understanding of purchasing decisions
[13] [16].

Using the Stimulus-Organism-Response theory, researchers de-
veloped a conceptual framework incorporating social-related fac-
tors, marketing-related factors, website-related factors, consumer
characteristics, and urgency to buy. These elements act as stimuli,
influencing the organism (consumer) and generating responses that
lead to impulse buying behavior in online shopping.

2 THE PRESENT STUDY
2.1 Social-related Factor and Impulse Buying
Consumer purchasing decisions are often influenced by social fac-
tors, including peer opinions, recommendations, and experiences
shared through electronic word of mouth (eWOM) and social media
[10]. Social influence affects consumer behavior, shaping beliefs,
attitudes, and purchasing decisions [1] [10]. Peer influence plays
a significant role, as individuals tend to align their choices with
their social circles [12]. Additionally, word-of-mouth marketing
remains a powerful tool in consumer decision-making, as personal
recommendations from trusted sources enhance product credibility
and encourage impulse buying [13]. Research highlights, that so-
cial interactions, particularly through peers and digital platforms,
drive impulse buying behavior by reinforcing trust and perceived
product value [15].

H1: Social-related factors have a significant effect on impulse

buying

2.2 Marketing-related Factor and Impulse

Buying

Impulse buying is an unplanned purchase driven by spontaneous
decisions. Marketers used factors like discounts, promotions, and
product appeal to trigger these impulses [14]. Studies show that
discounts significantly influence impulse buying, though some find-
ings suggest otherwise, highlighting a research gap [17] [23]. Pro-
motions such as price cuts, coupons, and flash sales enhance con-
sumer interest, often leading to impulsive purchases [5] [16]. With

2.3 Social-related Factors and Website-related

Factors

Social-related factors significantly influence website-related factors
by shaping consumer perceptions and interactions in digital en-
vironments. Li (2024) emphasized that product recommendations
from trusted video creators foster emotional connections, enhanc-
ing consumer trust and purchase intent, whereas recommenda-
tions from less favorable sources may lead to product rejection
[13]. Engaging advertisements and new media marketing strate-
gies subtly reshape consumer preferences, reinforcing purchase
motivation [20]. As website experiences are influenced by social
interactions, including peer influence, social presence, and cultural
context, which affect perceived usefulness, ease of use, and over-
all engagement [10], these findings underscore the importance of
integrating social elements into website design to optimize user
experiences and strengthen consumer trust [24].

H3: Social-related factors have a significant effect on website-

2.4 Marketing-related Factor and Consumer

Characteristics

Marketing-related factors influence consumer characteristics by
shaping perceptions of discounts and promotions. Emotion-driven
consumers see discounts as enhancing satisfaction, while concerns
over quality and authenticity can discourage purchases [14] [20]).
As for income levels affect discount tolerance, but product type and
necessity also play key roles [19].

H4: Marketing-related factors have a significant effect on con-

sumer characteristics

2.5 Website-related Factors and Urgency to Buy
The growing prevalence of e-commerce has underscored the signif-
icance of website-related factors in driving purchase urgency. Re-
search shows that website design elements, including high-quality
visuals, structured layouts, and scarcity-driven mechanisms such
as countdown timers and limited-stock notifications, significantly
influence consumer decision-making [9]. A well-designed website
creates trust and enhances the perceived value of urgency cues, en-
hancing impulse buying behaviors through the fear of missing out
[13] [22]. As digital commerce continues to evolve, the strategic in-
tegration of these elements remains essential in shaping consumer
purchasing patterns.

H5: Website-related factors have a significant effect on urgency

to buy

2.6 Consumer Characteristics and Urgency to

Buy

Impulse buying urgency is influenced by consumer characteristics
such as impulsivity, shopping lifestyle, and fashion involvement.
Highly impulsive individuals are more susceptible to purchasing

triggers, while trend-driven consumers respond quickly to time-
limited offers and product scarcity [9] [17]. The need to align
with evolving fashion trends further amplifies this urgency [20]
[25]. While strategic urgency tactics can drive impulse purchases,
excessive pressure may diminish consumer satisfaction and long-
term engagement [17].

Hypothesis 6: Consumer Characteristics has a significant effect

on Urgency to Buy

2.7 Urgency to Buy and Impulse Buying
Urgency, whether real or artificial, drives impulse buying by trigger-
ing emotions like fear of missing out and anticipated regret [6] [9].
Time-limited offers, scarcity cues, and price fluctuations heighten
this effect, pushing consumers toward quick, emotion-driven pur-
chases [5]. Retailers strategically use these tactics, particularly in
fast fashion and digital commerce, to accelerate decision-making
and increase sales [9].

Hypothesis 7: Urgency to Buy has a significant effect on Impulse

Buying

2.8 Website-related Factors and Impulse Buying
The evolution of digital markets has enhanced accessibility, influ-
encing impulse buying through website-related factors such as ease
of use, accessibility, and seamless payment [24]. Intuitive naviga-
tion and frictionless transactions reduce cognitive effort, increasing
unplanned purchases. However, some studies suggest that exces-
sive ease may diminish excitement, reducing impulsive tendencies
[17]. While individual consumer traits influence responses, website
design remains a key driver of impulse buying behavior.

Hypothesis 8: Website-related Factors has a significant effect

on Impulse Buying

2.9 Consumer Characteristics and Impulse

Buying

Shopping lifestyle strongly influences impulse buying, with fre-
quent shoppers and those who see shopping as entertainment be-
ing more prone to unplanned purchases [20]. Online browsing,
tailored ads, and promotions further drive impulsive decisions [20].
Hedonic shopping motivation also plays a key role, as consumers
seek emotional gratification through spontaneous buys [20]. While
higher income increases purchasing power [19], financial literacy
and budgeting habits help moderate impulse buying [2].

Hypothesis 9: Consumer Characteristics has a significant effect

on Impulse Buying

2.10 Website-related Factor as Mediator of

Social-related Factor and Impulse Buying

Website-related factors enhance impulse buying by improving us-
ability, convenience, and emotional engagement. A well-designed
website with intuitive navigation, fast-loading pages, and seam-
less e-payment systems encourages spontaneous purchases [23]
[24]. With positive user experiences drive offline word-of-mouth,
it enhances impulse buying behavior [24]. Aligned with the SOR
framework, website quality strengthens social influence, leading to
unplanned purchases [10].

3 METHODOLOGY
3.1 Design and Instrument
This study utilized a quantitative research design to examine the
factors influencing impulsive buying behavior. By applying a cor-
relational approach, the research aimed to determine the relation-
ships between demographic variables, social-related factors, and
website-related factors in driving impulsive purchases. This method
provided valuable insights into consumer behavior, contributing to
a deeper understanding of online shopping behavior in Cabuyao,
Laguna.

Researchers employed a structured, self-administered question-
naire distributed both online and in person. The survey was divided
into multiple sections, covering demographic characteristics and
key behavioral factors. It included seven variables, measured using
a five-point Likert scale using google forms for digital distribution,
ensuring convenience, accessibility, and broad participant reach.

3.2 Sampling Technique and Sample Size
Purposive sampling was utilized in this study to ensure the selection
of participants who met specific criteria relevant to the research ob-
jectives. This non-probability sampling method allowed researchers
to target individuals whose characteristics aligned with the study’s
focus on impulsive buying behavior. Participants were selected
based on age groups—21–30 (Gen Z) and 31–40 (Millennials)—and
their experience with online shopping and website usability.

A total of 381 respondents from Cabuyao, Laguna were surveyed,
exceeding the minimum requirement of 59 based on Cohen’s Table,
with an effect size of 0.25, an alpha level of 5%, and a statistical
power of 80% [8]. Data collection was conducted through Google
Forms and QR codes to ensure accessibility. Confidentiality was
strictly maintained in compliance with the Data Privacy Act of
2012, enhancing the reliability and ethical integrity of the study.

3.3 Statistical Treatment
The study utilized Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Model-
ing (PLS-SEM) to analyze structural relationships among variables,
particularly in cases with small sample sizes and non-normal data
distributions. WarpPLS 8.0 software was used to validate and assess
the reliability of the measurement model. The evaluation included
Composite Reliability (CR) and Cronbach’s Alpha (CA), ensuring
construct reliability, as well as convergent and discriminant validity
tests to confirm the clarity and accuracy of latent variables.

Convergent validity was assessed through the Average Variance
Extracted (AVE), requiring values above 0.5 for acceptance. Discrim-
inant validity was evaluated using the Fornell-Larcker criterion,
ensuring that each variable was distinct. Additionally, the Variance
Inflation Factor (VIF) was examined to detect multicollinearity, with
an acceptable threshold of 3.3 to prevent common method bias. R-
squared values greater than 0.5 indicated strong explanatory power,
confirming the model’s effectiveness in predicting outcomes.

Discriminant validity is assessed according to a specific criterion
to validate its acceptability. The average variance extracted (AVE)
should have a square root greater than any correlations between the
latent variables. Thus, the values along the diagonal must exceed
the values in the rows to their left or right.

The Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) assesses multicollinearity in
structural models, where high correlations between independent
variables can affect model stability. A VIF below 3.3 indicates no
collinearity issues, while values under 10 suggest an acceptable
range [11]. The R2 values—0.754 for website-related factors, 0.510
for consumer characteristics, 0.406 for urgency to buy, and 0.804 for
impulse buying—demonstrate strong predictive accuracy, exceeding
Cohen’s (1988) threshold for large effects [8]. Moreover, the Stone-
Geisser test (Q2) confirms the model’s predictive relevance, as all
values are above zero.

4 RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
The study confirms significant relationships between social-related
factors, marketing-related factors, website usability, urgency to buy,
and impulse buying. However, hypotheses H1, H2, H8, H12, and
H13 are rejected. The findings highlight the role of website-related
factors and consumer characteristics in influencing urgency and
impulse buying. For significance, p-values must be below 0.01,
and effect sizes indicate varying strengths of relationships. The
mediation model results further validate indirect effects on impulse
buying.

A final model (2) that removes factors with statistically insignifi-
cant results is the result of social-related factor, marketing-related
factor, website-related factor, consumer characteristics, urgency to
buy, and impulse buying.

5 CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATIONS
This study assessed the impact of social, marketing, and website-
related factors, consumer characteristics, and urgency to buy on
impulse buying in online shopping. Findings indicate that mar-
keting and social factors do not directly drive impulse buying but
influence consumer characteristics and website usability, respec-
tively. Website-related factors significantly affect urgency to buy,

which directly predicts impulse buying. Consumer characteristics
also play an important role which emphasizes the importance of
individual shopping behaviors. However, age and gender were not
found to moderate the urgency-impulse buying relationship.

These findings highlight the role of website usability and
urgency-driven strategies in enhancing impulse purchases. So-
cial factors attract consumers to platforms, but purchase decisions
are largely shaped by website experience and time-sensitive pro-
motions. Based on the findings, it is recommended that businesses
should focus on seamless website navigation, multiple payment
options, and real-time stock updates to encourage impulse pur-
chases. Marketers should utilize personalized promotions, such as
flash sales and limited-time offers, while ensuring strategic prod-
uct placements and clear discount visibility. Additionally, website
developers should prioritize usability enhancements to create an
engaging and frictionless shopping experience.

Furthermore, future researchers should explore broader demo-
graphic and cultural contexts, as well as additional marketing fac-
tors like social media advertising and personalized recommenda-
tions. Investigating psychological factors such as self-control and
emotional responses could further deepen understanding of impulse
buying behaviors. By optimizing e-commerce strategies based on
