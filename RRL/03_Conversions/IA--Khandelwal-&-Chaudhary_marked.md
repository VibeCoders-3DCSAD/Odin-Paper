Abstract
This research delves into the synergy of machine learning, computer vision, and color psychology in UI/UX design.
Advancements like convolutional neural networks have reshaped image-related tasks, while UI/UX design relies on
colors not just for aesthetics but also as strategic tools for communication and emotional resonance. Bridging the gap
between machine learning's analytical prowess and color psychology's nuances, the study explores how colors impact
user experiences, serving as navigational cues and emotional triggers. Motivated by technology's integration into daily
life, the research seeks to understand how algorithms process colors and influence user perceptions. Navigating
through the historical evolution of machine learning, UI/UX design principles, and color psychology, this condensed
exploration serves as a roadmap for readers, prompting contemplation on the possibilities when algorithms intersect
with human emotions within UI/UX design.
