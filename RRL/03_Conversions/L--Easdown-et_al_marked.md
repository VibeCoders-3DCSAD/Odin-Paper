ABSTRACT:
This study focuses on how working professionals' investments in higher education affect
economic dynamics as it examines the complex relationship between higher education and the
circular flow of income in the Philippine context. Using the preferred reporting items for
Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) methodology, the systematic literature
review approach employed and evaluated contemporary academic articles and empirical
investigations from 2018 onwards to provide a thorough and organized analysis. The study
emphasizes how higher education can improve one's own financial security while alsofostering
more economic growth through improved investment, saving, and consumption patterns. The
results show that greater levels of education promote improved finances and economic
engagement, which in turn maintains the circular flow of income and increases the resilience of
the national economy. The study emphasizes the value of curriculum that support working
professionals'educational advancement, suchas Outcomes-BasedEducation (OBBE) andOutput
Based Teaching and Learning (OBTL), as a tactical way to lessen economic inequality and
advancesustainableeconomicdevelopment inthe Philippines.

METHODOLOGY
This literature review on Harmonizing Work and Higher Education: Analysis of the
Circular Flow of Income in the Philippine Setting was conducted using a systematic approach.
The researchers focused on the studies related to the circular flow of income and the use of
OBTL curriculum. The utilization of reliable academic databases such as Google Scholar and
ssrn.com was conducted to identify studies and literature pertaining tothestudy. Thesystematic
review focused onharmonizingworkand highereducationandthe circularflowof incomeinthe
Philippines. To ensure a reliable and conscientious approach to the systematic review process,
the Preferred Reporting Itemsfor Systematic Reviewsand Meta-Analyses(PRISMA) framework
will be utilized. This framework helped the researchers to filter literature reviews, scholarly
journalsand academicstudies related totheirtopic.
The research strategy was further refined by conducting targeted searches in specific
academic databases to locate pertinent articles that discuss the impact of higher education on
economic patterns within the Philippine context. Keywords related to economic impact, such as
"economic growth", "income distribution", and "professional development", were used to
identify relevant studies. This approach ensured a focused and relevant collection of data,
enhancing the depth of the literature review. Each identified article underwent a rigorous
screening process to confirm its direct relevance and contribution to our understanding of how
higher education can influence economic conditions, particularly inadevelopingcountrysetting
like thePhilippines.
Inclusion Criteria:
The inclusion criteria for the literature review included studies about higher education
and circular flow of income that were published from 2017 up to the present. Local literature
publications were given priority since the researchers’ focus is onthecircularflow ofincomein
the Philippine setting. Various academic papers were considered such as academic articles,
research studies, dissertation, literature reviews and journal articles to determine the impact of
highereducation ontheeconomicactivity ofthe Philippines.

ExclusionCriteria:

CircularFlow of Income in thePhilippines
This study provides insights into how the circular flow of income is impacted by the
educational attainment of Filipino professionals. Those with higher education playacrucial role
in stimulating the economy through increased consumption, savings, and investments. They
demonstrate advanced financial literacy, engaging in diversified economic activities and
sophisticated investments that produce a multiplier effect, enhancing overall economic activity
(Suhendra etal.,2020).
Circular flow of income model is used as a measurement to the income of a certain
nation. Cleaver (2013) noted in hisstudy thatthe sectors inthecircular flowof incomehavetwo
roles intheeconomy - giverand receiver.Onemakesthepaymentandthe other onereceivesit.
Maharjan (2018) underscores the utility of the circular flow of income model in
illustrating the intricate movements of economic value within an economy, a sentiment echoed
by Raworth (2017) who highlights its role in delineating essential macroeconomic concepts.
According to Capa et al. (2023), this model also vividly depicts the exchange flows among
households, businesses, government institutions, and foreign sectors, which are pivotal to
understanding market dynamics. Hall & Klitgaard (2018) emphasize that the value of these
exchanges is critical, as they consist of goods and services that hold perceived value andcan be
monetized.
The World Social Report (2020) and Qazi et al. (2018) suggest that income disparities
significantly shape household consumption patterns, where greater inequality often restricts
spending to basic necessities, limiting discretionary spending. Meaning, the active participation
of educated professionals in the economy not only advances their financial status but also
solidifies the national economy's structure. The study reaffirms the pivotal role of higher
education in strengthening economic dynamics and underscores the necessity for policies that
