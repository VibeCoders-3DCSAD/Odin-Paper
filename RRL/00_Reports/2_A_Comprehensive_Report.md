## The Concept of Utang Na Loob Among Filipino Working Millennials
**Filename:** `L--Donato-et_al_summarized.md`

**ID:** `10.55927/fjss.v2i3.4572`  
**Odin Topics:** 1.C, 2.A, 13.B  

**TLDR:** Utang na loob among Filipino working millennials is an evolving value perceived as obligation, kagandahang loob, and love for family, manifesting as financial support and experiential reciprocity, with both fulfillment and burden.

**Problem & Motivation:** Studies on utang na loob are outdated and lack empirical research on Filipino millennials, who face pressures to support families while achieving career ambitions. Existing literature does not adequately capture how modern working millennials perceive and experience this cultural debt of goodwill. Understanding these perceptions is crucial for contextualizing Filipino financial behaviors in contemporary settings.

**Approach (summary):**
- Qualitative basic design with 30 Filipino working millennials aged 26-41 from Tuguegarao City, Cagayan.
- Purposive sampling recruited via Facebook and Messenger; one-on-one in-person or online semi-structured interviews.
- Thematic analysis following Braun and Clarke's six steps: familiarization, coding, theme generation, review, definition, and writing.

**Key Findings (selected):**
- Utang na loob is perceived as an obligation, but also as kusang loob (free will) and pakikipagkapwa (shared identity).
- Providing financial support to family is the primary manifestation, leading to increased work motivation and fulfillment.
- Millennials experience burden and compromised well-being due to strong family obligations, sometimes unable to save for future.
- Reciprocity extends to workplace colleagues and mentors through helping behaviors and loyalty.

**Limitations / Contradictions for Odin:**
- Sample limited to one city in the Philippines, limiting generalizability to other regions.
- Qualitative methodology does not quantify the prevalence or magnitude of financial burden.
- No longitudinal data on how utang na loob perceptions change over time [unacknowledged].

**Key takeaways:**
- Utang na loob motivates Filipino millennials to provide financial support to family.
- Reciprocity extends beyond family to mentors, colleagues, and strangers.
- Experiential gestures like vacations are preferred over material gifts.
