# Budgeting Practices Among Below Minimum Wage Earners: A Case Study of Sales Personnel of Local Enterprises in Pagadian City

Christine C. Alvarina¹, Harold O. Handumon², Joana Marie B. Peralta³, Marlon A. Paguia⁴, and Mylene P. Alfanta⁵  
¹,²,³ CBE Department, Saint Columban College, Pagadian City, Philippines  
DOI: https://doi.org/10.55248/gengpi.6.0325.11142

## Abstract

This study examines budgeting practices among wage personnel earning below minimum wage. The research was conducted in local enterprises within Pagadian City. This study aims to find out the budgeting practices for managing limited financial resources. A case study type of qualitative research approach was used for this study as it involves an in-depth understanding of the topic and helps diverse information gathered from the respondents. Findings reveal that besides the salary as their main source of income, the individuals also supplement their income through various techniques such as government financial assistance, online selling, selling thrift items, and selling side dishes. Budget allocations regularly cover basic necessities, such as utilities, rent, food, childcare, and daily allowances. Respondents face significant challenges, including managing numerous expenses, limited healthcare access, and insufficient financial savings. In response, they employ strategies like tight budgeting and prioritizing essential expenses. Despite their financial struggles, the individuals demonstrate admirable budgeting practices, emphasizing the importance of tracking expenses, utilizing resources, and establishing emergency financial savings. This study highlights the ability and adaptability of below minimum wage earners in navigating their financial situation, providing valuable insights for policymakers and practitioners in developing better programs and policies that better protect and support the rights of the workers.

**Keywords:** Sales personnel, Below minimum wage, Budgeting practices

## 1. Introduction

Budgeting is a way of estimating income and costs, helping people and companies manage their financial assets effectively. It allows monitoring of spending, debt management, and saving for future needs, improving earnings and financial savings. Budgeting is especially crucial for people with limited resources, as it provides a roadmap for managing expenses and achieving financial goals (White & Conde, 2024).

However, budgeting can be difficult for many because of income volatility and unsustainable living (Paul, 2019). Employment, particularly when it provides a stable income, plays a key role in achieving financial stability, but many low-paid people, particularly those in the low-income group, face financial constraints that make budgeting difficult.

The minimum wage varies across regions, with Pagadian City offering a daily rate between PHP 351 and PHP 381. Despite these changes, many people in low-paying jobs struggle to meet their basic needs, which hampers economic activities like spending and saving (Santos & Reyes, 2020). This, combined with inflation, reduces purchasing power and exacerbates financial stress. Sales personnel, often earning near or below minimum wage, face significant financial difficulties, making budgeting practices essential in understanding their financial challenges. More research is needed to explore how these individuals manage their finances, allocate their budgets, and address inflation, which can inform the development of more effective financial strategies and wage suggestions tailored to their needs (Jones & Brown, 2019).

## 2. Review of Literature

Budgeting is a technique of estimating income and expenses for specific functions and durations. Any entity uses it to spend money for their needs or desires, such as governments, families, companies, and even individuals who have generated income (Gani, 2024). The Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) indicated in the year 2023 that about 8% of Filipino workers are underpaid, which equals tens of millions of people facing financial challenges despite relentlessly working full-time. Even with the country’s economic growth, there is still a significant number of workers who are affected by low-paying jobs that fail to provide sufficient income for basic needs for their families. Thus, underpaid employment delays economic prosperity by limiting economic activities among consumers such as spending and saving, which are critical in maintaining economic growth (Santos & Reyes, 2020). In budgeting practices, it is essential to have a source of income that helps achieve financial stability. According to Boumahdiimad (2023), jobs significantly impact how our lives and communities are shaped. It profoundly affects people's well-being, societal advancement, and economic stability; they are more than a way to make a living.

The amount of compensation paid to an individual significantly affects how they handle budgeting and manage their finances (Bai, 2023). According to Maslow's hierarchy of needs, people will allocate their spending towards essential needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare before considering any other needs. In the case of underpaid workers, having a limited income may lead them to a tight budget, which requires them to prioritize important needs over unnecessary spending. Financial stress theory proves that lower salaries worsen financial stress, which impacts decision-making and requires cautious budgeting strategies to maximize limited resources.

## 3. Methodology

The participants of this study must be currently working as sales personnel of local enterprises in Pagadian City and who are below minimum wage earners. 10 individuals were selected based on their voluntary agreement to take part in the study. This research aims to understand how below minimum wage earners or underpaid workers handle their financial struggles and what financial budgeting practices they do as below minimum wage earners or underpaid workers.

The researchers were the main instrument of the study, guided by the interview questions approved and validated by the adviser and panelists to obtain information from the respondents. The researchers' actual instrument used by the participants in the interview questions were: What are the sources of income for the below minimum wage earners? What are the budget allocations for their compensation? What are the challenges they faced in budgeting as below minimum wage earners? What are the coping mechanisms and adaptive solutions used by the workers to manage financial challenges? What are their insights in managing their financial resources? The researchers recorded the whole discussion to get the respondents' answers. After collecting data, the researchers started analyzing the data at the end of the participants' interviews. Thematic analysis method was applied in analyzing qualitative data.

## 4. Findings and Conclusion

The research findings from the study reveal sources of income for the below minimum wage earners, budget allocations for their compensation, challenges they faced in budgeting, coping mechanisms and adaptive solutions used by the individuals to manage financial challenges, and insights in managing their financial resources. Using an interview guide, the researchers interviewed 10 participants who are below minimum wage earners. It shows that the sources of income of minimum wage earners include: (5) individuals who received government financial assistance, (3) individuals who engaged in online selling as part of their source of income, (1) participant responded selling thrift items, and (1) participant responded selling side dishes to earn additional income. The budget allocations for minimum wage earners' compensation include basic necessities: (6) participants allocate for utilities, (3) participants opted for rent payment, (9) participants allocate for food, (10) participants allocate for childcare, and (2) participants allocate their budget for daily allowance. The various challenges encountered by the respondents include: (5) participants struggling with budgeting itself from having different expenses to allocate, (3) participants experiencing limited access to healthcare, and (2) participants suffering from insufficient budget for savings. Moreover, findings on coping mechanisms for financial management reveal that the participants have different strategies and practices; those who prioritize housing, food, and healthcare experience lower levels of financial stress and improved general well-being. Positive insights in managing their financial assets amidst earning below minimum wage reveal that participants have remained positive and remarkable in managing their assets: (3) participants create emergency funds, (4) participants utilize resources, and (4) participants track their expenses, significantly improving their financial well-being despite having a low income.

The authors concluded that the budgeting practices among below minimum wage earners in Pagadian City reveal the resourcefulness and versatility of salespersons in the face of financial difficulty. Despite their limited income, minimum wage earners engage in diverse income-generating activities to supplement their income. Their budget allocations mainly include essential living costs and prioritization of basic needs in their financial planning. However, participants experienced significant challenges, including the difficulty of managing multiple expenses, limited access to healthcare, and inadequate savings. In response, they have developed effective coping strategies, such as tight budgeting and prioritizing essential expenses. Their positive insights on budgeting practices underscore the importance of tracking expenses, optimizing resource use, and establishing emergency funds for future purposes. Moreover, this study illustrates that even among low-income individuals, proactive financial management is possible through leveraging adaptive strategies and focusing on their essential needs, thus demonstrating admirable ability to navigate their financial circumstances, providing valuable lessons for financial literacy and supporting initiatives aimed at the low-income workforce.
